## Preamble

This document defines the **taxonomic architecture** of Commons Engineering — the structural logic by which 100,000+ patterns are organised, discovered, navigated, and qualified across four domain scales and six orbital layers.

It is not a catalogue. It is a gravitational map.

The taxonomy serves three purposes:

1. **Migration** — importing existing pattern knowledge from external traditions and prior repositories into a clean, IP-free, structurally coherent library.
2. **Generation** — creating new patterns at every orbital layer with consistent character, density, and clean room compliance.
3. **Detection** — recognising emerging patterns in the wild and placing them correctly within the gravitational field.

Every script in `_scripts/`, every enrichment prompt, every domain taxonomy spreadsheet, and every consulting engagement traces back to the architecture described here. If this document and a script disagree, this document governs.

## §1 The Gravitational Model

### §1.1 Two Opposing Forces

The Commons Engineering pattern library is not a hierarchy. It is a gravitational field with two opposing forces:

**Centripetal force (Orbit 0)** pulls patterns inward — toward unity, toward first principles, toward the universal. Orbit 0 is the singularity: infinite density, the centre of gravity that holds the entire system together. It is the force that says: *all patterns are, at their deepest level, expressions of the same physics of living systems.*

**Centrifugal force (Orbits 4–5)** pulls patterns outward — toward specificity, toward domain expertise, toward the gravity of extraction and specialisation. These are the patterns shaped by industry tradition, regulatory constraint, and operational necessity. They are useful precisely because they are specific. But left unchecked, their gravity pulls practitioners away from the commons and toward fragmentation.

Every pattern in the library exists in the tension between these two forces. Its orbital layer describes where it currently sits. Its gravitational hubs describe which centres of gravity it orbits. Its lifecycle describes whether it is moving inward (qualifying toward universality) or outward (specialising toward context).

### §1.2 Orbit 0 Creates the Force, Orbit 1 Forces the Structure

This is the foundational dynamic:

- **Orbit 0** is not a layer of content to be read and understood. It is the *attractor*. It generates the gravitational field that holds every other orbit in place. Without Orbit 0, the library is just a database. With it, the library is a living system with a centre.

- **Orbit 1** translates that force into structure. The ~100 First Principles and Practices at Orbit 1 are the bones of the system. Every domain, every value stream, every operational pattern must be traceable back through an Orbit 1 principle.

The relationship is not sequential (first 0, then 1, then 2...). It is simultaneous. Orbit 0 creates the *why*. Orbit 1 creates the *what*. Everything else is *how*, at varying levels of specificity.

### §1.3 Polycentricity

The gravitational field is not mono-centric. GraphRAG analysis reveals that the pattern library has **multiple centres of gravity** — polycentric structure in the sense Ostrom described for governance, but applied to knowledge.

A pattern like "Quarterly Capacity Alignment" (Orbit 4, Business) may orbit:
- `core_resilience` (Orbit 1) — because capacity alignment is fundamentally about system resilience
- `metabolic_renewal` (Orbit 1) — because it is a rhythmic resource renewal mechanism
- `bus_workforce_planning` (Orbit 3) — because it lives in the workforce planning domain

These multiple gravitational attachments are not noise. They are the **cross-domain geometry** that makes the library more than a filing system. The `gravitational_hubs` field in Pattern Spec v8.2 captures this explicitly.

## §2 The Six Orbital Layers

### §2.0 Orbit 0 — Singularity

**Character:** Infinite density. The source. The black hole at the centre.

**What lives here:** The irreducible principles from which all pattern knowledge derives. Not many — perhaps a handful. Life, entropy, coherence, the engineering attitude itself.

**Gravity:** Maximum. Orbit 0 generates the centripetal force that holds the system together. Every pattern in the library, regardless of its orbital layer, is ultimately a manifestation of Orbit 0 physics.

**Example:** `pat_core_00_life` — the pattern that states: vitality is the capacity of a system to regenerate itself through use.

**Current inventory:** ~7 patterns in `_patterns/_core/`

### §2.1 Orbit 1 — Principle (First Principles & Practices)

**Character:** Structural. The bones. Universal truths that hold across every domain scale.

**What lives here:** The ~100 principles and practices that are true for organisms, enterprises, cities, and ecosystems alike. These are not abstractions — they are design constraints. "Every living system must manage entropy" is an Orbit 1 principle. "Feedback loops must close within the system's metabolic cycle" is an Orbit 1 practice.

**Gravity:** Strong. Orbit 1 forces the structure onto everything below it. No Orbit 3 or 4 pattern is valid unless it can trace its lineage to at least one Orbit 1 principle.

**Relationship to Orbit 0:** Orbit 0 creates the *force*. Orbit 1 *forces the structure*. If Orbit 0 says "life", Orbit 1 says "therefore: entropy management, metabolic renewal, feedback closure, boundary permeability, adaptive capacity..."

**Alignment:** The Universal Taxonomy (§3) is aligned to Orbit 1. The four Universal Dimensions (Purpose, Agents, Offers, Operations) are Orbit 1 structural principles that manifest differently at each domain scale.

**Target inventory:** <100 patterns

### §2.2 Orbit 2 — Commons (The Attractor State)

**Character:** A commons systematically creates value that is alive, shared, just, and is built to last. The state every living system is being designed toward.

**What lives here:** Patterns that describe how systems create, distribute, and sustain value as commons. Not governance alone — governance is one aspect. Orbit 2 encompasses:
- **Collaborative architectures** — how multiple agents co-create value
- **Resource stewardship** — how shared resources are maintained and renewed
- **Distributed decision-making** — how governance emerges from participation
- **Value distribution** — how created value flows back to all contributors
- **System resilience** — how the whole sustains itself through disruption

**Critical distinction:** Orbit 2 is NOT just Ostrom's governance principles. It is the full spectrum of *what it means for a system to function as a commons*. Ostrom's principles are a subset — one lens on one aspect of one orbit.

**Gravity:** This is the attractor state. The entire purpose of Commons Engineering is to move systems toward Orbit 2 — from extraction (Orbit 4/5) through domain expertise toward the commons attractor. A system that reaches Orbit 2 is a system that has learned to create value collaboratively, distribute it equitably, and sustain itself regeneratively.

### §2.3 Orbit 3 — Bridge (The Universal Cross-Domain Patterns)

**Character:** NEW universal patterns that bridge domains. Not patterns dragged up from Orbit 4.

**What lives here:** Patterns that are inherently cross-domain — they exist at the intersection of multiple domain scales and cannot be reduced to any single domain. Knowledge management, communication architecture, governance mechanisms, coordination protocols.

**Critical distinction:** Orbit 3 is called "The Bridge" because these patterns connect domains. They are not domain-specific patterns that happen to appear in multiple domains (those remain at Orbit 4 in their respective domains). Orbit 3 patterns are *constitutively* cross-domain — they lose their meaning if confined to a single domain.

**Example:** "Polycentric Governance" is an Orbit 3 pattern. It manifests differently in Business (board structure), Urban (municipal federalism), Ecology (watershed management), and Life (distributed cognition) — but the *pattern itself* is the bridge between all four.

**Relationship to Orbit 4:** Orbit 3 does NOT pull patterns up from Orbit 4. It generates NEW patterns that are visible only when you look *across* domains. The cross-domain geometry revealed by GraphRAG is the primary discovery mechanism for Orbit 3 patterns.

### §2.4 Orbit 4 — Domain (The Mechanical Substrate)

**Character:** Dry, process-oriented, mechanical. The legacy operating patterns that every organisation eventually needs. What a desperate manager types into Google at midnight.

**What lives here:** The 19,000+ domain-specific patterns that form the operational substrate of each domain scale. In Business: "Invoice Three-Way Match", "Quarterly Capacity Alignment", "Standardized Leave Accrual". In Urban: "Storm Drain Capacity Assessment", "Bus Route Coverage Audit". In Ecology: "Soil Carbon Baseline Measurement". In Life: "Sleep Hygiene Protocol".

**Character requirement:** Orbit 4 patterns are deliberately dry and mechanical. Their titles are 2–6 words, process-oriented, and sound like something a practitioner would actually search for. They are NOT poetic, NOT aspirational, NOT commons-flavoured. They describe the world as it operates today — the baseline from which transformation begins.

**Clean Room Principle:** Orbit 4 patterns are generated under strict clean room conditions (§5). They extract the underlying mechanical pattern from industry traditions but completely strip all proprietary codes, trademarked terminology, and exact framework structures.

**Gravity:** The centrifugal force lives here. Orbit 4's gravity pulls practitioners toward specialisation, toward industry-specific expertise, toward the comfort of established frameworks. This is not wrong — it is necessary. But it must be balanced by the centripetal pull of Orbits 0–2.

**Current inventory:** ~19,000 Business patterns, ~14,500 Urban patterns, ~2,000 Life patterns, growing.

### §2.5 Orbit 5 — Edge (The Raw Frontier)

**Character:** Raw data, historic substrate, emerging anomalies, experimental ideas.

**What lives here:** Unvalidated patterns. Signals from the field that don't yet have enough evidence to qualify for Orbit 4. Historic patterns that may no longer apply. Anomalies detected by sensing systems that may represent genuinely new pattern types.

**Relationship to the pipeline:** Orbit 5 is the entry point. New patterns arrive here (from migration, from field sensing, from community contribution) and must qualify inward through evidence accumulation. The staging area `_patterns/_emerge/` holds Orbit 5 patterns.

**Gravity:** Maximum centrifugal. Orbit 5 is the edge of the system — the boundary between the pattern library and the raw, unstructured world of practice. Its gravity is the gravity of extraction and legacy: the tendency of knowledge to remain specific, contextual, proprietary, and siloed.

## §3 The Universal Taxonomy

### §3.1 The 4×4 Cross-Taxonomy Matrix

The taxonomy is organised as a matrix of **4 Universal Dimensions** (Orbit 1 structural principles) × **4 Domain Scales** (the living system scales Commons Engineering serves).

|                    | **Business** (Enterprise) | **Urban** (Settlement) | **Ecology** (Planetary) | **Life** (Organism) |
|--------------------|---------------------------|------------------------|-------------------------|---------------------|
| **PURPOSE**        | Strategy & Governance     | Vision & Planning      | Ecosystem Purpose       | Identity & Meaning  |
| *Definition & Purpose* | Portfolio, Profit, System, Board | Urban Vision, Land Use, Mobility Plan, Civic Identity | Biodiversity Goals, Climate Strategy, Conservation Mandate, Stewardship Charter | Purpose Discovery, Values Alignment, Life Design, Legacy Planning |
| **AGENTS**         | People & Partners         | Citizens & Institutions | Species & Actors       | Self & Relationships |
| *Participation & Relationship* | Hire to Retire, Community, Collaborate to Automate, Welcome to Transition | Residents, Civic Orgs, Public-Private, Migration & Integration | Keystone Species, Pollinators, Decomposers, Human Stewards | Inner Development, Family Systems, Social Networks, Professional Identity |
| **OFFERS**         | Products & Services       | Services & Infrastructure | Ecosystem Services    | Skills & Contributions |
| *Proposition & Exchange* | Discover to Usage, Lead to User, Distribute to Market, Customer Value Exchange | Public Services, Housing, Education, Healthcare | Carbon Sequestration, Water Purification, Soil Fertility, Biodiversity Banking | Knowledge Work, Creative Output, Care Work, Community Service |
| **OPERATIONS**     | Run & Maintain            | Operate & Sustain      | Cycles & Maintenance   | Habits & Metabolism  |
| *Production & Resilience* | Acquire to Retire, Plan to Fulfill, Source to Pay, IT & Maintenance | Utilities, Waste Management, Transport Ops, Building Maintenance | Nutrient Cycling, Hydrological Cycle, Seasonal Migration, Decomposition | Sleep & Recovery, Nutrition, Exercise, Cognitive Maintenance |

### §3.2 The Four Universal Dimensions

These four dimensions are Orbit 1 structural principles. They are true at every scale:

**PURPOSE — Definition & Purpose** — Every living system defines what it is and why it exists. The ongoing process of drawing boundaries between self and environment, and the state of having direction. At Business scale: strategy, portfolio, governance. At Urban scale: vision, spatial planning, civic identity. At Ecology scale: ecosystem function, biodiversity targets. At Life scale: purpose, values, meaning.

**AGENTS — Participation & Relationship** — Every living system is composed of participants — human, digital, biological, institutional — who take part in its life and form relationships that sustain it. The ongoing process of taking part, and the state of being connected. At Business scale: employees, partners, communities, automated systems. At Urban scale: residents, institutions, civic organisations. At Ecology scale: species, populations, human stewards. At Life scale: the self, relationships, networks.

**OFFERS — Proposition & Exchange** — Every living system puts something forward and exchanges it with its environment. The apple on the tree is a proposition; seed dispersal is the exchange. At Business scale: products, services, customer journeys. At Urban scale: public services, housing, infrastructure. At Ecology scale: ecosystem services (carbon, water, soil, biodiversity). At Life scale: skills, creative work, care, contribution.

**OPERATIONS — Production & Resilience** — Every living system must produce and sustain itself. The ongoing process of making, and the state of being able to persist, adapt, and transform. At Business scale: supply chain, fulfilment, IT, asset management. At Urban scale: utilities, waste, transport, maintenance. At Ecology scale: nutrient cycles, hydrology, decomposition. At Life scale: sleep, nutrition, exercise, cognitive renewal.

### §3.3 Taxonomy Alignment to Orbit 1

The four Universal Dimensions are not an arbitrary classification. They are the Orbit 1 expression of the fundamental physics of living systems:

- **PURPOSE (Definition & Purpose)** derives from the Orbit 0 principle that every living system defines itself — drawing a boundary between self and environment, establishing coherence and direction.
- **AGENTS (Participation & Relationship)** derives from the Orbit 0 principle that life is collaborative — no living system exists alone; all value creation requires participation and relationship.
- **OFFERS (Proposition & Exchange)** derives from the Orbit 0 principle that living systems put forward propositions and exchange with their environment — from the apple tree's fruit to the enterprise's value proposition.
- **OPERATIONS (Production & Resilience)** derives from the Orbit 0 principle that living systems must produce and sustain themselves — without continuous production and the resilience to persist through disruption, every system decays.

This alignment means the taxonomy is not just a filing system. It is a navigational instrument. When a new pattern is detected in the wild, the taxonomy tells you not just *where to put it* but *what gravitational forces are acting on it*.

## §4 The Value Stream Hierarchy

### §4.1 The Full Hierarchy

From most abstract to most concrete:

```
Stakeholder
  → Value Proposition
    → Journey
      → Touchpoint
        → Valuestream
          → Capability
            → Solution
              → Organisation (Team)
```

Patterns inform **Capabilities** within **Valuestreams**. The pattern library is the design knowledge layer that tells you *how* a capability should work, not *what* the capability is.

### §4.2 Structural Layers

**Universal Dimension** (4) — Purpose, Agents, Offers, Operations
  ↓
**Segment** (4 per dimension = 16 total) — e.g., within Purpose: CEO Office, CFO Office, CIO/CTO Office, Board Governance
  ↓
**Value Stream Family** (~3–4 per segment = ~48–64 total) — e.g., Purpose to Portfolio, Portfolio to Profit, Portfolio to System
  ↓
**Value Stream / Domain** (63 in Business, mapped to PCF traditions) — e.g., Strategic Planning, Financial Reporting, Enterprise Architecture
  ↓
**Process** (multiple per domain) — the operational steps within a value stream
  ↓
**Pattern** (multiple per process) — the design knowledge that informs how processes should work

### §4.3 Value Stream Family Naming Convention (Business Domain)

Business domain Value Stream Families use a deliberate "X to Y" naming convention:

**PURPOSE segment:**
- Purpose to Portfolio (CEO perspective)
- Portfolio to Profit (CFO perspective)
- Portfolio to System (CIO/CTO perspective)
- Board-Level Governance

**AGENTS segment:**
- Hire to Retire (talent lifecycle)
- Participants to Community (stakeholder engagement)
- Collaborate to Automate (human-machine coordination)
- Welcome to Transition (onboarding through offboarding)

**OFFERS segment:**
- Discover to Usage (product/service lifecycle)
- Lead to User (customer acquisition and retention)
- Distribute to Market (channel and market access)
- Customer Value Exchange

**OPERATIONS segment:**
- Acquire to Retire (asset lifecycle)
- Plan to Fulfill (demand-to-delivery)
- Source to Pay (procurement cycle)
- Predictive Maintenance & IT Operations

This naming is deliberately business-flavoured for Orbit 4 — it helps classic organisations find their place within the taxonomy. The same structural logic applies at other domain scales (Urban, Ecology, Life) with domain-appropriate naming.

### §4.4 The 63 Business Domains

The 63 functional domains at the Value Stream / Domain level are derived from PCF traditions but are **clean room extractions** — no proprietary codes, no trademarked process hierarchies. They represent the mechanical operating reality of enterprise: the things every organisation must eventually do, regardless of ideology.

The full domain list is maintained in `_taxonomy/business-engineering-domain-taxonomy-v2.xlsx` and is the source of truth for all generation, mapping, and enrichment scripts.

Each domain has:
- A domain number (1–63)
- A domain name (e.g., "Strategic Planning", "Payroll Management")
- A dimension assignment (one of the six dimensions, being consolidated to four Universal Dimensions)
- A pattern count (from existing mapping + Orbit 4 generation)

## §5 The Clean Room Principle

### §5.1 Why Clean Room

The pattern library draws on deep familiarity with industry frameworks: APQC PCF, ITIL, PMBOK, TOGAF, SCOR, SAFe, ISO standards, Lean/Six Sigma, and many others. These frameworks contain genuine insight about how organisations operate.

However, the frameworks themselves are proprietary. Their codes, their exact process hierarchies, their trademarked terminology, and their structural arrangements are intellectual property. Commons Engineering cannot build a commons on proprietary foundations.

The Clean Room Principle resolves this: **extract the underlying mechanical pattern, completely strip the proprietary wrapper.**

### §5.2 Clean Room Rules

**In Sections 1–5 of every pattern (Context, Problem, Solution, Implementation, Consequences):**

1. **NEVER** output framework codes (e.g., "11.2.3.1", "PCF 9.1.2", "ITIL 4.3.1")
2. **NEVER** use trademarked jargon verbatim:
   - Instead of SAFe "Agile Release Train" → "Synchronized Cross-Team Delivery"
   - Instead of TOGAF "Architecture Development Method" → "Iterative Architecture Cycle"
   - Instead of SCOR "Plan-Source-Make-Deliver-Return" → "Demand-Supply-Production-Distribution-Returns Cycle"
3. **NEVER** reproduce exact framework hierarchies or process decompositions
4. **ALWAYS** translate proprietary concepts into generic, dry, searchable mechanical terms
5. **ALWAYS** ground patterns in observable operational reality, not in framework theory

**In Section 6 (Known Uses):**

Framework references are permitted under Fair Use as academic observation:
- "This pattern is commonly implemented in organisations following service management traditions" (acceptable)
- "ITIL 4 Practice: Service Level Management, Process 4.3.1" (NOT acceptable — too specific)
- "Enterprise architecture frameworks describe a similar iterative cycle for technology governance" (acceptable)

**In the `grounding` field of Orbit 4 generation prompts:**

Name the framework *tradition* as academic context, but never cite specific proprietary codes:
- "Industrial HR compliance tradition" (acceptable)
- "APQC PCF 9.1.2.3" (NOT acceptable)
- "Service management best practice tradition" (acceptable)
- "ITIL 4 Practice Guide p.47" (NOT acceptable)

### §5.3 Clean Room Verification

The `build_combined_excel.py` script includes automated clean room verification via the `clean_grounding()` function, which strips residual proprietary references. Pattern enrichment scripts must include similar verification before writing final pattern files.

The following regex patterns are applied to detect violations:
- APQC codes and references
- SAFe trademarked terms (with word-boundary matching to avoid false positives like "safety")
- TOGAF-specific terminology
- SCOR model references
- Framework-specific process codes (e.g., "11.2.3.1" format)

## §6 The Orbital Transition Model

### §6.1 From Baseline to Commons

The orbital architecture is not just a classification — it is a **transition model**. Systems move through orbits:

**Orbit 5 → Orbit 4:** Raw signals qualify into recognised domain patterns through evidence and validation.

**Orbit 4 → Orbit 3:** Domain-specific patterns reveal cross-domain geometry through GraphRAG analysis. When the same structural pattern appears across Business, Urban, Ecology, and Life — but cannot be reduced to any single domain — a new Orbit 3 Bridge pattern is created.

**Orbit 3 → Orbit 2:** Bridge patterns that demonstrate resilient, collaborative, just, systematic value creation qualify as commons patterns. This is the hardest transition — it requires evidence that the pattern sustains itself regeneratively, not just that it appears everywhere.

**Orbit 2 → Orbit 1:** Commons patterns that prove universal across all domain scales — that are true for organisms, enterprises, cities, and ecosystems — distil into First Principles.

**Orbit 1 → Orbit 0:** The rarest transition. When a principle proves so fundamental that it cannot be decomposed further — when it is the irreducible physics of living systems — it joins the singularity.

### §6.2 The Blueprint Connection

This orbital transition maps directly to the Blueprint methodology used in consulting engagements:

- **Baseline** = Orbit 4 (where the system is today — mechanical, legacy, operational)
- **Near Future** = Orbit 3 (where the system can reach by bridging domains and building cross-functional capability)
- **Distant Future** = Orbit 2 (the commons attractor — where the system creates, distributes, and sustains value as a commons)

The consulting question is always: *how do you move a system from its current orbit toward the commons attractor, one capability at a time, without breaking what works?*

## §7 Pattern Detection and Taxonomy Navigation

### §7.1 Placing a New Pattern

When a new pattern is detected (from field observation, community contribution, literature review, or anomaly sensing), the taxonomy provides a deterministic placement algorithm:

1. **Domain Scale:** Is this pattern about enterprises (Business), settlements (Urban), planetary systems (Ecology), or organisms (Life)?
2. **Universal Dimension:** Does it primarily concern Purpose, Agents, Offers, or Operations?
3. **Value Stream Family:** Which family umbrella does it belong to?
4. **Domain:** Which of the 63 (Business) or equivalent domain-scale domains does it serve?
5. **Orbital Layer:** Is it a raw signal (5), a mechanical operating pattern (4), a cross-domain bridge (3), a commons pattern (2), a first principle (1), or irreducible (0)?
6. **Gravitational Hubs:** Which Orbit 0–2 principles does it orbit?

Steps 1–4 determine *where it goes*. Steps 5–6 determine *what forces act on it*.

### §7.2 Emerging Pattern Detection

The taxonomy aligned to Orbit 1 enables **emerging pattern detection**: when field signals cluster in ways that don't match existing patterns, the taxonomy structure tells you *what kind of pattern is missing*.

If GraphRAG reveals a cluster of relationships between patterns in "Hire to Retire" and "Collaborate to Automate" that don't map to any existing Orbit 3 Bridge pattern, that's a signal: a new cross-domain pattern may be emerging at the intersection of human talent lifecycle and human-machine coordination. The taxonomy doesn't just organise what exists — it reveals what's missing.
