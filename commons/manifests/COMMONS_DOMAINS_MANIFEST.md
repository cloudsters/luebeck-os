## Preamble: The Chart, Not the Territory

A sea chart does not own the ocean. It does not own the ships that sail on it, the harbours they visit, or the cargo they carry. It shows what is there — because the ships themselves broadcast their position, course, and character. The chart renders what is already being sent.

commons.domains is the sea chart of the commons world.

Every Commons OS instance that chooses to be visible broadcasts into the shared protocol: its purpose, its propositions, its capabilities, its ecological parameters, its contact endpoint. commons.domains renders those broadcasts into a navigable, layered chart — so that commons can find each other, participants can discover commons worth joining, and the world can see what the commons economy looks like at any given moment.

commons.domains does not register commons. It does not approve them. It does not hold their data. It receives their broadcasts and renders them. When a commons goes dark, it disappears from the chart. When a new commons lights its beacon, it appears. The chart is always current because the broadcasts are continuous. No one maintains it. It maintains itself.

## S1 What commons.domains Is

commons.domains is a **multi-layered rendering and communication surface** for the commons world. It has three functions:

**Rendering.** It visualises the broadcasts of all commons instances that choose to be visible — their positions (geographic and topological), their purposes, their propositions, their connections, their ecological parameters. The visualisation is layered: different layers show different aspects of the same world.

**Discovery.** It enables commons to find each other — by purpose, by geography, by domain, by capability, by ecological zone. Discovery is not search in a database. It is tuning into the broadcasts that match what you are looking for. Like a radio scanning frequencies.

**Communication Bridge.** It provides a surface where commons that have discovered each other can initiate contact — not through commons.domains as intermediary, but through direct peer-to-peer connection. The chart shows where the other ship is. The communication happens ship-to-ship. commons.domains is the introduction, not the conversation.

## S2 Layers

The sea chart has many layers. Each layer renders the same broadcasts through a different lens. Some layers exist from the start. Others will emerge as the commons world grows.

### S2.1 The Reality Layer

The default layer. Renders broadcasts from real-world commons instances — organisations, cities, ecosystems, and individuals operating the Commons OS against real problems with real stakes. This is the live chart of the actual commons economy.

### S2.2 The Simulation Layer (commons.games)

Renders broadcasts from simulated commons instances operating in the commons.games world. Same broadcast protocol, same rendering, different reality. The simulation layer is visually distinguishable from the reality layer — you always know which world you are looking at.

commons.games provides the simulation engine, the world parameters, and the AI agents. commons.domains provides the visualisation. The simulation layer on the chart is the window into the games world.

### S2.3 Domain Layers

Thematic layers that filter by application domain:

- **Energy** — all commons broadcasting energy-related intents
- **Food** — all commons broadcasting food/agriculture intents
- **Mobility** — all commons broadcasting transport/logistics intents
- **Care** — all commons broadcasting health/care intents
- **Skills** — all commons broadcasting learning/capability intents
- **Ecology** — all commons broadcasting ecological parameters
- **Maker** — all commons broadcasting fabrication/repair intents

Domain layers are not curated. They emerge from the broadcast metadata. When enough commons broadcast intents tagged "energy," the energy layer appears. The chart renders what exists.

### S2.4 Federation Layers

Layers that show the connections between commons — federation memberships, active A2A handshakes, intent-matching flows, trust relationships. Where the domain layers show nodes, the federation layers show the network.

### S2.5 Future Layers

The architecture is open. Any layer that can be derived from broadcast data can be added:

- **Historical** — time-lapse of how the commons world evolved
- **Ecological** — overlay of ecological parameters and their effect on commons operations
- **Impact** — aggregate impact metrics across commons
- **Tension** — where conflicts, scarcity events, or triage situations are active

## S3 What commons.domains Is Not

**It is not a registry.** No one registers. Commons broadcast. The chart renders.

**It is not a platform.** It holds no data, mediates no transactions, extracts no rent. The broadcasts are the commons' own. The chart is a view, not a container.

**It is not a marketplace.** Intent matching happens through the Commons MCP, peer-to-peer. The chart shows that intents exist. It does not match them.

**It is not a governance layer.** It has no authority over the commons it renders. It cannot approve, reject, suspend, or modify any commons. It is a mirror, not a regulator.

**It is not a centralised service.** The rendering can be replicated by anyone with access to the broadcast protocol. commons.domains is one rendering surface. Others can exist. The protocol is open. The broadcasts are public (for those who choose to broadcast). Anyone can build a chart.

## S4 The Broadcast Protocol

commons.domains depends entirely on the Broadcasting capability of the Commons OS. The chart can only render what is sent. The Broadcasting specification lives in the Commons OS Manifest and Specification — not here. This manifest describes what the chart does with the broadcasts, not how the broadcasts work.

What the chart needs from the protocol:

| Broadcast Element | What the Chart Renders |
|---|---|
| Position (geographic + topological) | Where the commons appears on the map |
| Purpose (D1) | What the commons stands for — its beacon |
| Propositions (D3) | What the commons offers and needs — its signals |
| Capabilities (D4) | What the commons can do — its capacity |
| Participants (D2) | Who is part of the commons — its community |
| Ecological Parameters | The ecological context the commons operates in |
| Federation Memberships | Which networks the commons belongs to |
| Contact Endpoint | How to reach the commons directly |
| Broadcast Level | How much is being shared (Beacon → Signal → Open → Transparent) |

A commons broadcasting at **Beacon** level appears as a point of light with a purpose label. A commons broadcasting at **Transparent** level appears as a rich, navigable profile with live governance telemetry.

## S5 The Communication Bridge

Discovery is only useful if it leads to connection. commons.domains provides a communication bridge — not as a messaging platform, but as a protocol for initiating peer-to-peer contact.

When a commons discovers another through the chart, it can:

1. **View** — see whatever the other commons is broadcasting (always available)
2. **Ping** — send a structured introduction ("We are X, we are interested in Y, here is our credential") through the broadcast protocol
3. **Connect** — if the ping is accepted, both commons' Blueprint MCPs establish a direct peer-to-peer connection. From this point, communication is direct. commons.domains is no longer involved.

The bridge is a handshake facilitator, not a communication channel. Like a harbour master who introduces two captains and then steps back.

## S6 Relationship to commons.games

commons.games and commons.domains share:

- The same broadcast protocol
- The same rendering architecture
- The same layer concept

They differ in what they render:

| | commons.domains | commons.games |
|---|---|---|
| What it shows | Real-world broadcasts + simulation layer | The simulation world in detail |
| Who operates the commons | Humans + AI | AI (humans steward) |
| Depth of detail | Chart-level (overview, discovery) | World-level (internal operations, scenarios) |
| Time | Real-time | Accelerated or real-time |

commons.games is both a layer on the commons.domains chart AND a standalone world with its own depth. On the chart, you see the simulated commons as points of light. Inside commons.games, you see their internal operations, their scenarios, their failures and recoveries.

The chart provides the bird's-eye view. The game provides the street-level view.

## S7 Open Architecture

commons.domains is open infrastructure:

- The broadcast protocol is open and specified in the Commons OS.
- The rendering engine is open-source.
- The chart data is derived from public broadcasts — anyone can build their own rendering.
- The communication bridge protocol is open.

There is no vendor lock-in, no proprietary data, no competitive moat. commons.domains is useful because it is the first and best-known rendering of the commons broadcast protocol. If someone builds a better chart, the commons world benefits. The protocol remains the same. The broadcasts remain the same. Only the view changes.

## S9 Relationship to Other Manifests

commons.domains is the visibility layer of the Commons Engineering ecosystem:

- **Commons OS Manifest** — defines the Broadcasting capability that commons.domains renders. The chart is only as rich as the protocol allows. Broadcasting specification lives in the OS, not here.
- **Commons Games Manifest** — defines the simulation world that appears as one layer on the chart. commons.games provides the simulation engine; commons.domains provides the visualisation.
- **Commons Blueprint Manifest** — every commons that broadcasts has a Blueprint. The chart may render Blueprint metadata (purpose, timeslice, governance health) for commons broadcasting at Open or Transparent level.
- **Commons Agent Manifest** — the four agents of each commons produce the governance telemetry that feeds the trust dimensions visible on the chart.
- **Commons Place Manifest** — commons.domains is itself a Place (digital, persistent, governed). The chart is the Marketplace pattern at global scale — where the commons world meets anyone who wants to see it.
- **Commons Incubator Manifest** — Incubators help new commons light their beacons. commons.domains makes those beacons visible to the world.
